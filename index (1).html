Beef Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 2 tablespoons water (optional, if using flour)

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the beef cubes and brown them on all sides.
  3. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  6. Return the browned beef to the pot.
  7. Season with salt, pepper, paprika, and cumin. Stir well to coat the beef.
  8. Pour in the beef broth. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat, cover, and let it cook for at least 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  9. (Optional) If you want a thicker gravy, in a small bowl, whisk together the flour and water until smooth. Gradually stir this mixture into the simmering stew. Cook for a few more minutes until the gravy has thickened.
  10. Serve hot.

Rice Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups water or broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (optional)

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps to remove excess starch.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, water or broth, and salt.
  3. (Optional) Add butter or oil for extra flavor and to prevent sticking.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
  5. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan tightly with a lid, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until all the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender. Do not lift the lid during this time.
  6. Once the rice is cooked, remove it from the heat and let it stand, covered, for 5-10 minutes. This allows the steam to finish cooking the rice and makes it fluffier.
  7. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving.
